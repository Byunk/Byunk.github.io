## SAP

I started my career at SAP. I am living in Seoul and October is my 4th month after leaving home Daejeon and KAIST. As I have not yet graduated from school, my current routine involves working three times a week and attending school twice a week. One of the greatest things I have gained from my experience at SAP is the wonderful colleagues.

There are ~25 VT/STAR interns and they all have different backgrounds. The point is making interactions with them both enjoyable and comfortable. The fact that there are people I feel comfortable talking to within the company is really big deal. And there are great seniors as well.

I still remember this one time when Jeongyeon Na, a COOP intern from SNU, did a presentation about her internship work. Following her presentation, some of the senior colleagues engaged in a discussion regarding the impact of the reasearch and limitations he though with very deep understanding. I was so suprised that how he and other seniors dove into a discussion, while I sat quietly in the middle of the room, feeling motivate by thier expertise. This experience inspired me to become a senior professional like them one day.

## Neovim

I have decided to switch my IDE from VSCode to Neovim for two main reasons. Firstly, I prefer working within the terminal environment rather than constantly switching between windows. With VSCode, I found myself writing code in the editor, then moving to the terminal to execute it, and then returning to VSCode, which became quite cumbersome and annoying. Secondly, Neovim is way more worderful than I initially thought. After configuring it to my preferences, I have become obsessed with its highly scalable features and the incredible range of plugins available. As I continue using Neovim, I'm also exploring other command-line tools such as `tmux`, [`lf`](https://github.com/gokcehan/lf), and [`todo.txt-sh`](https://github.com/todotxt/todo.txt-cli).
